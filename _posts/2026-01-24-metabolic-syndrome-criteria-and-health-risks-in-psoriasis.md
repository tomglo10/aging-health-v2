## Why Metabolic Syndrome Matters in Psoriasis

People with psoriasis are significantly more likely to develop **metabolic syndrome**, a condition that dramatically increases the risk of heart disease, stroke, Type 2 Diabetes, and premature death. Importantly, this risk is driven by chronic systemic inflammation, not lifestyle factors alone.

## What Is Metabolic Syndrome?

Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions that occur together. To be diagnosed, a patient typically meets **three or more** of the following criteria:

1.  **Abdominal Obesity:** A waist circumference of 40+ inches (men) or 35+ inches (women).
2.  **Elevated Blood Pressure:** 130/85 mmHg or higher.
3.  **High Fasting Glucose:** 100 mg/dL or higher.
4.  **High Triglycerides:** 150 mg/dL or higher.
5.  **Low HDL ("Good") Cholesterol:** Less than 40 mg/dL (men) or 50 mg/dL (women).

Combined, these factors create a "Perfect Storm" for your cardiovascular system.

## The Shared Inflammatory Pathways

Psoriasis promotes metabolic dysfunction through immune-driven inflammation. Cytokines such as **TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-17** act as messengers that:
* Promote **Insulin Resistance**, the precursor to Type 2 Diabetes.
* Disrupt lipid metabolism, leading to high triglycerides.
* Contribute to **Endothelial Dysfunction**, making arteries stiffer and more prone to plaque.

---

## Health Risks: Beyond the Skin

When psoriasis and metabolic syndrome coexist, the risks extend into every major system:

### 1. Cardiovascular Disease
Psoriasis patients with metabolic syndrome face accelerated atherosclerosis and a significantly higher incidence of myocardial infarction (heart attack).

### 2. Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/MASLD)
There is a direct link between psoriasis and liver fibrosis. Systemic inflammation makes the liver more prone to storing fat, which can lead to scarring if left unmanaged.

### 3. Type 2 Diabetes
The insulin resistance driven by [Type 3 Diabetes pathways]({{ '/posts/brain-health-is-alzheimers-really-type-3-diabetes/' | relative_url }}) is often amplified by the skin's inflammatory load.

> ### 🎯 March 2026 Clinical Update: Metabolic Targets
> * **Protein Support:** To maintain skin integrity and prevent the muscle wasting (sarcopenia) often seen in chronic inflammation, seniors must target **1.2–1.6 g/kg** of protein (USDA 2026).
> * **Vascular Integrity:** Maintain a blood pressure target of **<130/80 mmHg**. This protects the delicate micro-vasculature already under stress from metabolic syndrome.
> * **Inflammatory Monitoring:** If managing a psoriasis flare, monitor your glucose more closely, as inflammation can cause sudden "sugar spikes."

## 🧐 Metabolic Risk FAQ

#### Does clear skin mean the metabolic risk is gone?
No. Research shows that cardiovascular and metabolic inflammation can progress even when skin symptoms are well-controlled. Integrated management of both "internal" and "external" symptoms is essential.

#### Can lifestyle changes really help if it's inflammatory?
Absolutely. Weight management and low-glycemic eating patterns reduce the overall "inflammatory fire," making your psoriasis treatments more effective.

#### Should I see a specialist for this?
Patients with moderate-to-severe psoriasis should ideally be managed by a team that includes a dermatologist, a cardiologist, and potentially an endocrinologist to ensure all metabolic markers are monitored.
